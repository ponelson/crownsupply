Who supplies the crowned houses of the world.

A working ledger of tradespeople, tailors, breeders, gardeners, jewellers, and grocers who carry, or once carried, royal appointment. Curated for accuracy over flourish.

A note on state. Royal warrant regimes change. King Charles III completed a two-tranche review in 2024–25 that dropped roughly a hundred previous holders (Cadbury, Unilever, Krug among them) and reappointed 386 in December 2024, with William and Catherine joining as grantors from spring 2026. King Frederik X of Denmark announced in November 2024 that Danish warrants will be phased out by 2029 with no renewals. Spain, Norway, and Japan no longer grant formal warrants; Middle Eastern and most Asian monarchies have never used the system. Where no formal warrant exists, this ledger records known long-standing suppliers with a distinct status marker.

Method. UK data reflects the Royal Warrant Holders Association register as reissued by King Charles III and Queen Camilla through the 2024 and December 2024 tranches. Continental warrants sourced from Hofleverancier (NL), Hovleverantör (SE), Hoflev (DK), Cour Grand-Ducale (LU), and the Belgian Court supplier register. Middle Eastern and Asian entries reflect publicly reported long-standing supply relationships where no formal warrant system exists.

Limits. Warrants are granted to named individuals within firms and can be revoked or lapse. Personal suppliers to individual family members are often not disclosed. Nothing here should be read as a currently endorsed commercial claim; verify with the relevant royal household or association before citing.
